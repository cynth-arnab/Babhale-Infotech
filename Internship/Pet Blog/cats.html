The weird domestic creature

Cats are like tiny, mysterious software programs with unpredictable behaviors and a tendency to crash in the most unexpected ways. One moment they're purring in your lap, the next they're launching themselves off furniture in a chaotic burst of energy—like a program stuck in an infinite loop.

They have a love-hate relationship with gravity, sometimes sticking to high surfaces like a well-written process, other times defying physics with their bizarre mid-air maneuvers. Their debugging method? Randomly knocking things off tables to see what happens.

And let's not forget their security protocols—cats operate on a strict zero-trust model. They'll stare at you suspiciously for hours, testing your credentials with slow blinks, only to suddenly demand authentication in the form of belly rubs (which may or may not be a trap).

A cat is basically an open-source cryptid: available for study, but never fully understood.
