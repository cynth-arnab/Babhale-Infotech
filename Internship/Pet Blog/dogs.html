The non-weird domestic creature

Dogs are like well-optimized, user-friendly programs—reliable, predictable, and always eager to execute commands. Their default mode is loyalty.exe, running on an infinite loop with a subroutine for tail-wagging and face-licking.

Unlike cats, dogs operate on a full-trust model—no authentication required. Walk in the door? Immediate access granted with maximum enthusiasm. They are also excellent error handlers—drop food on the floor, and they’ll promptly clean up the mess, no questions asked.

In contrast to the cryptic nature of cats, dogs are open-source and well-documented—their emotions are clear, their intentions transparent, and their enthusiasm always at 100%. Basically, if cats are UNIX systems (powerful but demanding expertise), dogs are like a well-designed GUI—intuitive, friendly, and always eager to help.
